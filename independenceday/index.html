Independence Day of the USA

Indenpendence Day (also called Forth of July) is probably the most important federal holiday for Americans. 
It commemorates publication of American Declaration of Indenpendence in 1776.
Many people use it as opportunity to show their patriotism by participating in parades and ceremonies,
wearing clothes in their national colours and hanging out flags.

History

In 1775 people living in New England began fighting the British Kingdom for their indenpendence. 
On July 2, 1776 the Second Continental Congress secretly voted for indenpendence from Great Britain.
Two days later the final wording of the document was approved and it was published. On 2 August
delegates began to sign the Declaration of Indenpendence.

The Comitee of Five were men who drafted and presented 
Declaration of Indenpendence to the Second Continental Congress.
They worked from June 11 to the publication of the document.
Members of the Comitee of Five:

  • John Adams
  • Thomas Jefferson
  • Benjamin Franklin
  • Roger Sherman
  • Robert Livingston

    Customs

    In the summer of 1776 some colonist celebrated independence
of USA by holding mock funerals for King George III. People
organized many festives, parades, bonfires and firing of cannons
during public readings of Declaration of Indenpendence. Philadelphia
held the first commemoration of independence on July 4, 1777.
In 1778 Massachusetts became the first state celebrating July 4th as
an official state holiday.
Today Americans celebrate Indenpendence Day by participating in
public events and parades.

    



    Other activities associated with the day include
watermelon or hotdog eating competitions and sporting
events, such as baseball games, three-legged races,
swimming activities and tug-of-war games.

    



    Symbols of USA
    

    Flag
    


    It consists of thirteen equal horizontal red and white stripes
whith a blue rectangle bearing 50 white, five pointed stars.
Stars represents states of the USA and stripes represents 13 colonies
that declared indenpendence from the Great Britain.
First version with 13 stars was adopted in 1777 and the current one in 1960.
    Great Seal
    


    It is a seal used by the US federal goverment.
Design of the obverse of the seal is used on
United States passports, military insignia, embassy
placards, and various flags. It is also national 
coat coat of arms of the United States. It also
appears on the reverse of the one dolar bill.
Many goverment agencies use it's elements on their
seals.

    The Star Spangled Banner
    


    
Oh, say can you see, by the dawn’s early light,
What so proudly we hailed at the twilight’s last gleaming?
Whose broad stripes and bright stars, through the perilous fight,
O’er the ramparts we watched, were so gallantly streaming?
And the rockets’ red glare, the bombs bursting in air,
Gave proof through the night that our flag was still there.
O say, does that star-spangled banner yet wave
O’er the land of the free and the home of the brave?

On the shore, dimly seen through the mists of the deep,
Where the foe’s haughty host in dread silence reposes,
What is that which the breeze, o’er the towering steep,
As it fitfully blows, now conceals, now discloses?
Now it catches the gleam of the morning’s first beam,
In full glory reflected now shines on the stream:
‘Tis the star-spangled banner! O long may it wave
O’er the land of the free and the home of the brave.

And where is that band who so vauntingly swore
That the havoc of war and the battle’s confusion
A home and a country should leave us no more?
Their blood has wiped out their foul footstep’s pollution.
No refuge could save the hireling and slave
From the terror of flight, or the gloom of the grave:
And the star-spangled banner in triumph doth wave
O’er the land of the free and the home of the brave.

Oh! thus be it ever, when freemen shall stand
Between their loved homes and the war’s desolation!
Blest with victory and peace, may the heaven-rescued land
Praise the Power that hath made and preserved us a nation.
Then conquer we must, for our cause it is just,
And this be our motto: “In God is our trust.”
And the star-spangled banner forever shall wave
O’er the land of the free and the home of the brave!
